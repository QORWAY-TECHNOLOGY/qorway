# Workforce Capital

**QORWAY Decision Intelligence Infrastructure**

> Workforce capital is the governed capacity to reason about human capability, AI-assisted work, upskilling, productivity, oversight, and long-term organizational resilience.

---

## 1. Definition

QORWAY treats workforce transformation as a decision system problem, not only as a headcount, automation, or productivity topic.

In modern organizations, value creation increasingly depends on the interaction between human capability, AI-assisted work, supervision and oversight, upskilling investment, operational autonomy, productivity systems, governance requirements, and social or regulatory impact.

Workforce Capital is the public-safe domain concept that describes how QORWAY can reason about those interactions.

---

## 2. Role in QORWAY

Workforce Capital helps QORWAY model decisions such as where human capability must be strengthened, where AI agents may support work, where human oversight remains required, where automation creates operational or social risk, where upskilling is a capital allocation decision, and where workforce transition affects resilience, compliance, and execution quality.

This domain can interact with finance, CSRD/RSE, regulator, supply chain, and sovereign resilience contexts.

---

## 3. Architectural Position

Workforce Capital is represented as a Domain Pack category in the private architecture.

Publicly, it should be understood as a domain lens that connects:

```text
capability → decision quality → execution capacity → resilience → feedback
```

It does not replace HR systems.

It does not provide employment, tax, or labor-law advice.

It is a decision intelligence domain for workforce-related operating capacity.

---

## 4. Public-Safe Decision Questions

A Workforce Capital system may help organizations ask:

- Which capabilities are becoming critical to future decision quality?
- Which workflows require human oversight even when assisted by AI?
- Where does automation increase or reduce operational resilience?
- Where should upskilling investment be prioritized?
- Which workforce changes affect compliance, ESG, or continuity exposure?
- How should human-agent collaboration be governed and audited?
